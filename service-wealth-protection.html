Protecting What You’ve Worked Hard to Build

Growing wealth is only half the equation — protecting it is just as important. Wealth protection ensures that if life takes an unexpected turn, your family, business and long-term plans are not derailed. At Greystone Wealth Capital, we design integrated strategies that combine insurance, ownership structures and estate planning considerations to safeguard your financial position.

We look beyond standalone policies and products, focusing instead on how each element of your plan — superannuation, investments, business interests, personal cover and lending — works together to manage risk in a deliberate, tax-effective and sustainable way.

Our Wealth Protection Framework

Every strategy is tailored to your circumstances, risk profile and family structure, with a focus on maintaining control, flexibility and clarity at claim time or during major life events.

  • Coordination of Life, TPD, Trauma and Income Protection as part of a broader risk strategy.
  • Structuring cover ownership (personal, super, SMSF, business) to manage tax, control and beneficiary outcomes.
  • Key Person, Buy/Sell and business continuity strategies for owners and directors.
  • Reviewing binding death benefit nominations, beneficiary arrangements and alignment with your estate planning.
  • Integrating lending, investment risk and cash flow considerations into your protection plan.
  • Ongoing review to ensure cover levels, ownership and structures keep pace with changing wealth, family and business needs.

  • How is wealth protection different to just having insurance?

    Insurance is one tool; wealth protection is the strategy. We focus on how your cover, ownership structures, nominations and estate planning work together to protect your assets, income and beneficiaries in a coordinated way.

  • Do you work with my solicitor and accountant?

    Yes. We regularly collaborate with your legal and tax advisers to help ensure your structures, wills, trusts and nominations all support the same protection objectives and remain compliant with current regulations.

  • How often should my wealth protection plan be reviewed?

    We recommend at least an annual review, or sooner if you experience major changes such as a new property, business purchase or sale, marriage, children, or a significant increase in income or assets.
