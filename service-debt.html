Strategic Debt Reduction

The way your debts are structured can make a significant difference to your cash flow, interest costs and ability to build wealth. At Greystone Wealth Capital, we help you take control of home loans, investment loans, personal loans and credit cards with a clear, practical plan to pay them down faster — without compromising your lifestyle.

Our goal is to shift you from “managing repayments” to having a structured path toward being debt-free, while aligning your strategy with broader goals such as investing, protecting your family and preparing for retirement.

How We Help Reduce Your Debt

We analyse your full position and design a tailored debt strategy that prioritises smart structure, lower interest and disciplined progress, not shortcuts or quick fixes.

  • Review of all existing facilities: home loans, personal loans, credit cards and other commitments.
  • Debt consolidation strategies where appropriate to simplify repayments and reduce rates.
  • Structuring loans to distinguish between deductible and non-deductible debt.
  • Optimising loan features such as offsets, redraw and repayment frequency.
  • Cash flow planning so repayments are sustainable and aligned with your lifestyle.
  • A clear roadmap to accelerate paying off bad debt while still building long-term wealth.

  • Should I consolidate my debts?

    Consolidation can reduce interest and simplify repayments, but it isn’t always the right answer. We assess your situation to ensure that any consolidation improves your position rather than extending terms or increasing long-term interest.

  • How do I decide which debts to pay off first?

    We typically prioritise high-interest and non-deductible debts first, while considering your risk profile, goals and tax position. Your strategy is mapped out so you know exactly what to target and when.

  • Can I reduce debt and still invest?

    In many cases, yes. The right balance depends on your risk tolerance, time frame and existing commitments. We help determine when to focus aggressively on debt, when to allocate to investment, and how both can work together toward your goals.
