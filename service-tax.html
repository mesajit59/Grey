Smart, Strategic Tax Planning

Effective tax minimisation isn’t about aggressive schemes or shortcuts. It’s about using the rules correctly so more of what you earn is working for your future. At Greystone Wealth Capital, we work alongside your accountant to design strategies that align your income, investments, superannuation and structures in a tax-efficient way.

From contribution strategies and investment structuring through to retirement planning and estate considerations, our focus is to reduce unnecessary tax drag while keeping your strategy simple, compliant and aligned with your goals.

How We Help Minimise Your Tax

We review your full financial position and identify opportunities to improve the after-tax outcome of the decisions you are already making.

  • Superannuation contribution strategies (concessional, non-concessional, carry-forward rules).
  • Structuring investments across personal, super, company or trust ownership to improve tax efficiency.
  • Capital gains tax (CGT) planning, including timing of disposals and use of available discounts.
  • Tax-effective retirement income strategies using account-based pensions and transition to retirement.
  • Review of insurance ownership and premiums to optimise deductibility and cash flow.
  • Collaboration with your accountant to ensure your financial plan and tax position work together.

  • What does “tax minimisation” actually involve?

    It involves legitimately arranging your affairs so you do not pay more tax than required under current law. This may include optimising where assets are held, how income is received, and how super contributions and pensions are used.

  • Do you replace my accountant?

    No. We complement your accountant’s work. We focus on strategy, structures and forward planning within your broader financial plan and coordinate with your tax adviser to ensure consistency and compliance.

  • Is this type of advice only for high-income earners?

    Not at all. Smart tax strategies can benefit a wide range of clients — from professionals and families through to business owners and pre-retirees. Even small improvements, applied consistently, can have a significant long-term impact.
